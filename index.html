Understanding Sildenafil and Tadalafil

Both sildenafil and tadalafil belong to a class of medications known as PDE5 inhibitors. These drugs improve blood flow to the penis, helping men achieve and maintain erections during sexual stimulation.

Sildenafil (Viagra)

  • Onset: Usually works within 30–60 minutes
  • Duration: Effective for around 4–6 hours
  • Use: Ideal for planned sexual activity
  • Tip: Works best when taken on an empty stomach

Tadalafil (Cialis)

  • Onset: Usually starts working within 30–60 minutes
  • Duration: Can remain effective for up to 36 hours
  • Use: Provides flexibility and spontaneity
  • Daily Option: Low-dose daily treatment available
Because tadalafil lasts much longer than sildenafil, it is often called the “weekend pill.”

Factors to Consider When Choosing the Right ED Medication

1. Frequency of Sexual Activity

  • Sildenafil: Best for occasional use.
  • Tadalafil: Better for frequent or spontaneous intimacy.

2. Lifestyle and Convenience

Tadalafil allows more spontaneity because of its longer duration. Sildenafil is better for men who prefer taking medication only when needed.

3. Health Conditions

Men with cardiovascular disease, diabetes, or chronic health conditions should consult a licensed medical professional before starting ED medication. Telehealth platforms such as Adam Health provide online consultations with qualified clinicians.

4. Possible Side Effects

  • Headache
  • Flushing
  • Indigestion
  • Nasal congestion
  • Back pain (more common with tadalafil)
